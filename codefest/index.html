About the CodeFest

The CodeFest is a month-long competition between Conestoga students! You will be tasked to code any project you desire—be it game, app, or website. You can choose to work in a team of up to four, or go solo. At the end of the competition, you’ll be asked to share your projects with the club in a brief presentation.

The Rules

The rules of CodeFest are the following (Modified from MLH Guidelines):
  1. There will be a max of four members per team. If you form a large team and win a challenge, the winnings for each person could be less.
  2. Teams can of course gain advice and support from teachers. In other words, we do allow asking for advice, clarification, and language/syntax assistance from the computer science teachers Mr. Dewees and Mr. Sharick, non-participants from the club, or any other coding expert you know of. We also strongly encourage asking for help if you are stuck on coming up with ideas. However, all work must be your team’s. Experts can only give guidance and are prohibited from coding anything for you. Teams may not work with each other. Copying code of any kind is strictly prohibited.
  3. All work on a project should be done during CodeFest.
  4. Teams can use an idea they had before CodeFest.
  5. Teams can work on ideas that have already been done. Projects do not strictly have to be “innovative”. If somebody wants to work on a common idea they should be allowed to do so and should be judged on the quality of their project. These days it’s hard to find something that’s fully original and teams might not know an idea has been done before anyway.
  6. Teams can work on an idea that they have worked on before (as long as they do not reuse code).
  7. Teams can use libraries, frameworks, or open-source code in their projects. Working on a project before the event and open-sourcing it for the sole purpose of using the code during the event is against the spirit of the rules and is not allowed.
  8. Adding new features to existing projects is allowed. Judges will only consider new functionality introduced or new features added during the CodeFest in determining the winners.
  9. Teams must stop coding once the time is up. However, teams are allowed to debug and make small fixes to their programs after time is up. e.g. If during demoing your hack you find a bug that breaks your application and the fix is only a few lines of code, it's okay to fix that. Making large changes or adding new features is not allowed.
  10. Teams can be disqualified from the competition at the organizers' discretion. Reasons might include but are not limited to plagiarizing, unsporting behaviour, or breaking any other competition rules.

How will the projects be judged?

ORIGINALITY (20)

Is your project creative? If not wholly original, does it have elements of innovation? Does it have aspects not seen in any other current day product? 

PURPOSE (30)

Does your project meet your goals? Does the end product implement all the advertised functions? Is the project useful? (being entertaining counts as useful!)

POLISH (40)

How well does your project work? Does it even run? Do all functions work as intended?

COMPLEXITY (10)

How impressive is your code? Does it reflect your extensive knowledge of your chosen language? Does it involve lots of technical skill?

Note: the language you choose to code in does not affect this category. In other words, we don’t care if it takes you 20 lines to write a for-loop, but we do care if you used the language in a clever way. 

Why should I participate?

CodeFest is our annual Stoga hosted computer science competition. It aims to show that coding can be a fun and enjoyable activity– providing a different setting for programming than what you may be used to. And, since time is money, we want to offer real prizes for impressive efforts and incentivize putting forth your best. All the prizes will be supplied by the Conestoga Computer Science Club. We encourage everyone to participate, even those not confident in their coding skills. Working with friends on a fun project is valuable learning experience, and there will be a variety of prizes to ensure experienced coders don’t hog all the winnings!
